Antimicrobial Activity and Characterization of Silver Nanoparticles from Catharanthus roseus



Project Guide : Subhashini Jr

Department of Biotechnology School of Bioengineering 18BTP107L - Minor Project

The medicinal plants generally have a wide range of antimicrobial activities and many phytochemical properties owing to the presence of a number of active alkaloids, flavonoids, various amino acids and carbohydrates. Catharanthus roseus is one of the important medicinal plants, due to the presence of the indispensable anti-cancer drugs, i.e., vincristine and vinblastine. It has medicinal importance owing to the presence of alkaloids like ajamalicine, serpentine and reserpine, which are well known for their hypotensive and antipasmodic properties. This study focuses on the green synthesis of nanoparticle from the leaves and flower of Catharanthus roseus (Periwinkle) which has been proven against malaria parasite Plasmodium Falciparum. The production of AgNPs was visually detected by the colour changes of the reaction mixture from yellow to reddish brown. The UV-spectrum showed the plasmon resonance peak. The silver nanoparticles formed were predominantly cubical with uniform shape. The morphology of the nano particles has observed through Transmission Electron Microscopy.
